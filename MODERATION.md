# Moderation Policy

This document explains how moderation works in the Istanbul Digital Nomads community.

## Who Are the Moderators?

Moderators are trusted community members who help keep our spaces welcoming and safe. Currently, moderation is handled by the community organizers. As we grow, we'll invite active and trusted members to join the moderation team.

## What We Moderate

- **Spam and self-promotion:** Unsolicited ads, MLM schemes, and repetitive promotional posts will be removed.
- **Harassment and hate speech:** Any form of harassment, discrimination, or hateful content results in immediate action.
- **Scams and misleading content:** Fake job offers, phishing links, or dishonest business promotion will be removed and the poster banned.
- **Off-topic content:** We gently redirect conversations that don't fit the channel/topic they're posted in.

## How We Handle Issues

**Step 1: Assess.** When a report comes in or a moderator spots an issue, we look at the context and severity.

**Step 2: Act.** Depending on severity:
- Minor issues: A friendly reminder via DM
- Moderate issues: Content removal and a warning
- Serious issues: Immediate mute or ban

**Step 3: Document.** We keep a private log of moderation actions for consistency and transparency.

**Step 4: Follow up.** For warnings, we check in to make sure the issue is resolved.

## Reporting an Issue

If you see something that violates our Code of Conduct:

1. **In Telegram:** Message an admin directly (don't call people out publicly)
2. **On GitHub:** Open an issue in the community repo with the label "moderation"

All reports are confidential. We will never share who reported an issue.

## Appeals

If you believe a moderation action was unfair, you can message the community organizer directly to discuss it. We're human and we make mistakes too.

## Becoming a Moderator

Active community members who demonstrate our values may be invited to join the moderation team. There's no application process; we notice people who consistently help others and keep conversations healthy.
